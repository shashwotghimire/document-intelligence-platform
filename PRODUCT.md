# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

General professionals (lawyers, analysts, researchers, consultants) who need to query their own documents and get reliable, cited answers. They upload PDFs, Word docs, CSVs, and text files, then ask natural-language questions. The primary job is finding information inside their own content, fast, without re-reading everything.

## Product Purpose

documentGPT is a RAG-based document intelligence platform. Users upload documents, which are chunked and embedded as vectors. Chat queries perform semantic similarity search and stream LLM responses grounded in retrieved context. Success looks like: a user gets an accurate, sourced answer in seconds rather than reading a 40-page PDF.

## Brand Personality

Minimal, clean, focused. The product gets out of the way of the work. References: Perplexity and Claude.ai — AI-native surfaces where the conversation is the UI, chrome is invisible, and typography does the heavy lifting.

## Anti-references

- ChatGPT-style dark sidebar with endless chat list (overly familiar AI aesthetic, nothing distinctive)
- Generic SaaS dashboard (blue-heavy, metric cards, charts for the sake of charts)
- Heavy enterprise UI (dense tables, corporate gray, nav-heavy)

## Design Principles

1. **Content is the interface.** Every element that isn't document content or a response is a candidate for removal or reduction.
2. **Confident restraint.** Fewer elements, each doing more work. Empty space is not wasted space.
3. **Earned complexity.** Admin and power features exist but don't intrude on the primary reading/asking workflow.
4. **Typographic clarity.** Hierarchy through scale and weight, not decoration. Instrument Serif for moments of editorial presence; Inter Tight for function.
5. **Trust through precision.** Cited, grounded answers build trust. The UI should feel equally precise: nothing vague, nothing approximate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text). Reduced motion support for all transitions. No formal compliance deadline, but contrast and keyboard navigation are non-negotiable.
